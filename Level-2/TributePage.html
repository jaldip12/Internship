About Marie Curie

Marie Skłodowska Curie was a Polish and naturalized-French physicist and chemist who conducted pioneering research on radioactivity. She was the first woman to win a Nobel Prize and remains the only person to win Nobel Prizes in two different scientific fields—Physics and Chemistry.

Her discovery of the elements polonium and radium, along with her work in developing mobile radiography units during World War I, had a profound impact on science and medicine. Her tireless dedication to research opened doors for women in science and revolutionized our understanding of atomic structure.

Marie Curie's legacy lives on through her contributions to science, her role in shaping modern physics, and her inspiring perseverance in the face of numerous obstacles.
